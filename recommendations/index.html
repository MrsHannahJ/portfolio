Recommendations

Small businesses and non-profits have limited budgets; they need easy-to-manage solutions at reasonable costs. For that reason, most of my clients rely on Content Management Systems (CMS), like WordPress and Shopify. These systems allow them to adjust their site and it's content without having to hire a developer every time.

Even if we don't end up working together, you may find this list helpful. Contact me if you have any questions.

Hosting

Top Pick: BlueHost

It has all the standard bells and whistles, starts at less than $4 per month and COMES WITH a domain and SSL certificate. Plus, 24/7 customer service...yes, please! It is my go-to hosting provider.

If you are building a WordPress site and would like a WordPress-centric host, check out WP Engine. The costs are higher than Bluehost, but they offer month to month contracts and no sign-up fees.

E-Commerce

Top Pick: Shopify...then WooCommerce

For most e-commerce clients, I prefer Shopify because it integrates so seamlessly into so many internet marketing and sales channels. It also has an incredibly user-friendly interface to manage the store. While Woocommerce and other solutions exist, they can be more complicated for the typical non-developer.

Blogs & Small Business Websites

Top Pick: WordPress

WordPress is the world's most popular platform for websites and for good reason: it is just solid. It is especially perfect if you plan to operate a blog. Because it is so popular there is a huge user pool which means even if you've never used it before, there are tons of resources to learn from. There is a difference between WordPress.com and WordPress.org, see here for an explanation on the difference (and why I use WordPress.org when I build for clients).

Email Marketing

Top Picks: MailChimp & Send In Blue

For email marketing, I LOVE MailChimp. Awesome analytics, seemless integration with Shopify and other tools. However, if you have a large email list and a tight marketing budget, you may find that SendinBlue is a better bargain.

Online Learning

Top Picks: The Internet...Pluralsight

Let's face it - the internet is the best thing ever. There are so many tools and resources. Each tool usually has it's own documentation, so the first step should always be a Google search for whatever you're looking for. Check out YouTube, Udemy, Treehouse and more. My favorite source for tutorials on new web skills is Pluralsight. They offer tons of tutorials on various web technologies, CMS as well as web design, content creation and marketing. They'll even let you start with a free trial.

More Goodies

Here is a quick list of other tools you may need for producing a new website:

  • Website Tools? From analytics to advertising, page speed tests and more, Google is the best!

  • Free stock photography? Pexels
